Subhash Chandra Bose

Fearless Indian nationalist leader and freedom fighter, who played a pivotal role in the struggle for India's independence and led the Indian National Army (INA) against British colonial rule.

About the Legend

☛ Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, India.

☛ He studied at Cambridge University, England, and later passed the Indian Civil Service examination, but he resigned in 1921 to join the freedom movement.

☛ Bose initially joined the Indian National Congress but later became disenchanted with its methods and ideologies.

☛ Bose was arrested eleven times by the British authorities for his involvement in the independence movement.

☛ In 1939, he broke away from the Congress and formed the Forward Bloc, a group advocating aggressive nationalism.

☛ During World War II, Bose escaped house arrest and sought support from Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan to raise an army to fight against British forces.

☛ Bose formed the Indian National Army (INA) in 1942, which fought alongside Axis powers against the British in Southeast Asia.

☛ Bose established the Provisional Government of Free India (Azad Hind Government) in Singapore in 1943.

☛ Bose's wartime broadcasts from Radio Berlin and the Indian Legion inspired Indians to join the struggle for independence.

☛ The circumstances of Bose's death in a plane crash in Taihoku (now Taipei) on August 18, 1945, remain mysterious and have given rise to various conspiracy theories.

☛ Bose is remembered for his slogan "Jai Hind" and for his relentless efforts to free India from colonial rule.

☛ Despite INA's limited success on the battlefield, it significantly contributed to the momentum of the Indian independence movement.

☛ Bose is posthumously honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1992.

☛ Bose's military strategies and the INA's legacy influenced the development of the Indian armed forces post-independence.

☛ The circumstances surrounding Bose's death continue to be a subject of historical debate and investigation.

☛ Subhas Chandra Bose is celebrated as a heroic figure in India's struggle for independence, and his legacy remains influential in the country's history.
